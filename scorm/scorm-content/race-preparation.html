Entry and acceptance procedure

Horses don’t just turn up on the day to race. Trainers and owners want to select a race that their horse will do well in. Distance, age and weights all influence the choice of race and there is an entry and acceptance procedure to follow. This is the procedure for a standard Saturday raceday at Flemington. Some feature Group 1 races such as the Emirates Melbourne Cup, AAMI Victoria Derby and Crown Oaks have an extended nomination process with multiple declaration stages and fees, which is why the list of entries for these races can be released months in advance of the actual race.

The trainer must submit their entries by 12pm on the Monday before the raceday to Racing Australia.

The handicapper reviews the entries and assigns the weights later that day.

The trainer must then decide whether to accept for the race and advise Racing Australia by 9.30am on the Wednesday before the raceday, and confirm the jockey by 12pm that day.

 

If the trainer decides not to take part in the race after already accepting, they may scratch the horse by 7.30am on the day of the race for a small fee; after this, it is considered a late scratching and the penalty fee is doubled.
